About Me

I'm a computer scientist who believes the most elegant code is the kind that connects people to opportunity. My work is fueled by a simple, personal mission: to build technology that makes quality education feel less like a privilege and more like a right.

This isn't abstract for me. It's for my grandmother, whose own brilliant mind never got the chance to learn past grade school. It's for the teachers like Mr. Evans, who taught me that understanding is a superpower.

I choose Computer Science—not to chase the latest tech trend, but to acquire the ultimate toolkit for solving human problems. My approach is guided by wisdom from industry experts:

Fall in love with the problem, not the technology. For me, that means building something simple and robust that works on a cheap tablet in a remote classroom is more beautiful than the most complex AI.

Become a world-class communicator. I strive to be the person in the room who can passionately advocate for the user—whether that user is a struggling student or an overworked teacher—and translate their needs into technical reality.
